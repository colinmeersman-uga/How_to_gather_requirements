# Requirements Gathering Quickstart

Use this for small or low-risk requests.

The goal is not to collect perfect information. The goal is to gather enough information to define the next useful step, document assumptions, and identify what still needs to be clarified.

## Minimum Questions

1. What problem are we solving?
2. Who is the user or stakeholder?
3. What should happen when this is complete?
4. What system, record, file, or process is involved?
5. What are the acceptance criteria?
6. What edge cases or blockers do we already know about?
7. Who owns the next step?

## Minimum User Story Format

As a [user/persona], I want [action], so that [value].

## Minimum Acceptance Criteria

- Given [context], when [action], then [expected result].
- The request is complete when [testable condition].
- Known exceptions are documented.

## Output

At the end of lightweight requirements gathering, you should have:

- one user story
- clear acceptance criteria
- known open questions
- owner for next action
- priority or backlog placement

- ## The Analyst Mindset: Input, Process, Output

Requirements gathering is not taking an order. Do not simply write down the requested solution. Your job is to understand what information enters the process, what decisions need to be made, and what should happen at the end.

### 1. Input

Ask:

- What starts this process?
- What information, files, records, forms, or systems are involved?
- Do we have all the data needed to make the decision?
- Which information is reliable?
- Which information might be missing, inconsistent, or user-entered?

### 2. Process

Ask:

- What decisions need to be made?
- What rules determine those decisions?
- What data should those decisions be based on?
- Can we infer missing information?
- Can we safely use a default?
- What happens if we make the wrong decision?
- How would we detect that something went wrong?

### 3. Output

Ask:

- What should be created, updated, moved, sent, displayed, or logged?
- Who needs to see the result?
- Where should the result live?
- What proves that the request is complete?
- What should happen when the process cannot complete successfully?
